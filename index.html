Research Statement

I am Prof. Dr. R. Dinakaran Michael, formerly, Dean of

Life Sciences at Vels University, Chennai, India and

Head of PG Zoology, The American College, Madurai .

My research interests (Annexure 1) are in the field of

fish immunology, immunostimulation and

immunotoxicology. I have more than 25 years of

experience in directing the Research Centre for Fish

Immunology at various institutions in India. I have also

organized and participated in several international and

national conferences, workshops and training

programs on immunological techniques and

aquaculture.

My research aims to understand the immune system of

fish and its modulation by various natural and synthetic

compounds, as well as environmental factors such as

stress, pollution and pathogens. I have used different

fish models such as Oreochromis mossambicus,

Cyprinus carpi o, Channa striata and Labeo rohita to

study the effects of immunostimulants such as plant

extracts, azadirachtin and tannery effluent on their non-

specific immune responses and disease resistance. I

have also investigated the immunotoxic effects of

heavy metals such as chromium and cadmium on fish

immune cells and organs.

My research has contributed to the advancement of

knowledge in fish immunology and has implications for

aquaculture industry, environmental management and

perhaps human health. I have publ ished more than 50

peer-reviewed papers in reputed journals such as Fish

& Shellfish Immunology, Indian Journal of

Experimental Biology , Aquaculture Research and

Ecotoxicology and Environmental Safety. I have also

authored two books and several book chapte rs on fish

immunology. I have received many awards and

honours for my research achievements, such as the

UGC Emeritus Fellow, FAO Expert/Consultant for Fish

Immunology, Scientist Exchange Fellowship Award of

the Indian National Science Academy- DFG, Germany,

State Best Teacher Award of Tamil Nadu Government

and John Humphrey Fellowship of British Society for

Immunology.

I work ed on several research projects funded by

various agencies such as UGC, DST, DBT, OASTC

(MoES)

My recent projects were all on Immunostimulation by

marine algae in fish . Earlier I was looking at the

Immunotoxicity of heavy metals in fish.

I am also actively involved in teaching and mentoring undergraduate and postgraduate

students, as well as research scholars in the field of l Immunology. I have supervised

Annexure 1

Research Interest

The main areas of our research interest include developing environment -friendly

prophylactic and therapeutic immunostimulants from terrestrial and marine plants for

culture fish. Another area is heavy metal-induced immunosuppression in fish and this

aspect has relevance to extensive cultures in large water bodies which are more often

receive polluted waters form rivers and canals which are polluted by industrial

effluents. Stress-induced immunomodulation due to social stressors ( e.g., crowding,

monosex cultures) is another priority area of research in this centre.

Plant-derived immunostimulants

Aquaculture faces substantial annual loss of production due to disease s. Antibiotics

and chemo -therapeutic agents are being used to control microbial diseases in

aquaculture systems. These agents are not only expensive, but they are also

environmental pollutants. Further, they often result in the development of antibiotic

resistant strains of microbes. In this context, terrestrial and marine plant -derived,

immunostimulating compounds offer promising results due to its broad spectral

activity and cost -effectiveness. India has been bestowed with enormous wealth of

traditional t errestrial medicinal plants and marine plants. Hence the centre aims at

studying the immunostimulatory and antibacterial properties of these plants ’ extracts

in fish for developing the processes and the products for wider application of them as

immunoprophylactic and therapeutics in aquaculture in this country.

Heavy metal induced immunomodulation

Aquatic environment of fish is in close contact with numerous pollutants. Aquatic

pollutants such as heavy metals modulate (most of them suppress) the immune

system of fish, thus increasing the host susceptibility to infectious pathogens. So, one

of the aims of the centre is to investigate the effect of heavy metals such as chromium,

cadmium, mercury and other common pollutants on the immune status of fish. This

assessment of the immune status of fish is not only helpful as the source of basic

immunotoxicology information but also as immuno-indicators for monitoring fish health

in extensive fish cultures in large lakes and ponds.

Stress-induced immunomodulation

Intensive aquaculture practices where culture fish is usually overcrowded often result

in stress -mediated immunosuppression in fish, leading to sudden outbreak of fish

diseases. This laboratory aims at finding the mechanism behind stress –mediated

immunosuppression in fish. Another related area of interest is the effect of complex

social interactions among the culture fish communities (all male, all female or mixed

population) on the immune system of fish. This approach can help fish farmers to

device species-friendly management practices thereby preventing heavy loss due to

outbreak of diseases.
